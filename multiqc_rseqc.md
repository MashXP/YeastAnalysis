# RSeQC Analysis Report

## Overview
This report summarizes the RNA-seq quality control metrics generated by RSeQC for samples ERR458497-ERR458502.

## 1. Read Distribution
Analysis of read mapping to genomic features (CDS, 5'UTR, 3'UTR, Introns, Intergenic).

| Sample | CDS Exons (%) | Introns (%) | Intergenic (%) |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **ERR458497** | 94.7 | 0.05 | 5.27 |
| **ERR458498** | 94.6 | 0.05 | 5.33 |
| **ERR458499** | 94.7 | 0.05 | 5.29 |
| **ERR458500** | 91.1 | 0.10 | 8.77 |
| **ERR458501** | 91.1 | 0.10 | 8.82 |
| **ERR458502** | 91.1 | 0.11 | 8.82 |

**Key Findings:**
- **High Exonic Mapping:** All samples show >90% of reads mapping to CDS exons.
- **Low Intronic/Intergenic Signal:** Intronic reads are negligible (~0.05-0.1%), confirming successful polyA enrichment and minimal genomic DNA contamination.
- **Sample Grouping:** Samples ERR458500-502 show slightly higher intergenic background (~8.8%) compared to ERR458497-499 (~5.3%), potentially indicating a batch effect or biological condition difference, though still within very high quality limits.

## 2. Junction Annotation
Classification of splice junctions as Known (present in reference annotation) vs. Novel.

| Sample | Known Splice Events (%) | Novel Splice Events (%) |
|:---|:---:|:---:|
| **ERR458497** | 95.1 | 4.7 |
| **ERR458498** | 94.6 | 5.1 |
| **ERR458499** | 94.9 | 4.9 |
| **ERR458500** | 95.0 | 4.7 |
| **ERR458501** | 94.6 | 5.2 |
| **ERR458502** | 94.8 | 5.0 |

**Key Findings:**
- **High Annotation Concordance:** ~95% of splicing events match the *S. cerevisiae* reference annotations.
- **Novel Splicing:** The low rate of novel splicing events (~5%) is expected for a well-characterized model organism like yeast.

## 3. Gene Body Coverage
Reads were assessed for their distribution across the normalized gene length (0% = 5' end, 100% = 3' end).

**Key Findings:**
- All samples exhibit coverage across the entire gene body.
- Profiles show a typical bell-shaped or slightly 3'-biased distribution, which is standard for polyA-selected RNA-seq.
- No severe 5' degradation is observed (which would appear as a sharp peak only at the 100% mark).

## Conclusion
The RNA-seq library quality is **excellent**. The high exonic mapping rate and known junction consistency indicate high-quality RNA extraction and library preparation. The data is suitable for downstream quantification and differential expression analysis.
